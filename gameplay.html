Gameplay Highlights

This guide provides a quick overview of some core gameplay mechanics. These rules form the foundation for skills checks, combat, and action management during your game.

Skill Checks

Skill checks determine the success or failure of an action, based on the character's ability scores and proficiency.

  • Rolling a d20: Roll a 20-sided die (d20) and add the relevant ability modifier (e.g., Dexterity for Acrobatics).
  • Difficulty Class (DC): Compare the result to the DC set by the DM to determine success or failure.
  • Critical Success/Failure: For non-attack rolls, rolling a 1 or 20 is not automatically a fail or success unless the DM specifies otherwise.

Advantage and Disadvantage

Advantage and disadvantage represent favorable or unfavorable circumstances affecting a roll.

  • Advantage: Roll two d20s and use the higher result.
  • Disadvantage: Roll two d20s and use the lower result.
  • If both apply, they cancel each other out and you roll normally.

Combat Rules

Initiative

At the start of combat, each creature rolls a d20 and adds their Dexterity modifier to determine turn order.

  • Initiative Order: Players and NPCs take turns in descending order of initiative rolls.
  • Ties: Resolve ties by comparing Dexterity scores; the DM decides if scores are equal.

Action Economy

On their turn, a creature can perform actions based on the following categories:

  • Action: Standard activities like attacking, casting a spell, or using an ability.
  • Bonus Action: Quick actions granted by specific abilities, spells, or features.
  • Reaction: A single response triggered outside your turn, like an opportunity attack.
  • Free Actions: Simple tasks like drawing a weapon or speaking a few words.
